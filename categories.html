Categories of Sea Turtles

Green Turtle

(Chelonia mydas)

One of the most recognizable sea turtle species is the green turtle, or Chelonia mydas, which is distinguished by its striking green colouring and heart-shaped shell. It is a sizable marine reptile that may grow to a length of 5 feet and weigh up to 500 pounds. Throughout the world, tropical and subtropical waters are home to the green turtle, which is renowned for its protracted migrations between feeding and nesting areas. The green turtle is slowly making a comeback despite being categorized as endangered in many regions of the world due to issues including habitat loss and poaching. This is because of conservation efforts and protection measures.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

(Eretmochelys imbricata)

The severely endangered hawksbill turtle, or Eretmochelys imbricata, is a type of sea turtle renowned for its exquisitely patterned shell. It is a small turtle, usually reaching lengths between 2-3 feet and weighing up to 150 pounds. The warm waters of the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Seas are home to the hawksbill turtle, which eats sponges, sea anemones, and other invertebrates. The hawksbill turtle continues to be threatened by poaching, habitat loss, and climate change despite being legally protected in many nations. To prevent the extinction of this famous species, conservation activities are essential.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

(Dermochelys coriacea)

The largest and most distinctive species of sea turtle is the leatherback, sometimes referred to as the lute turtle. The leatherback gets its name from the fact that, in contrast to other sea turtles, it has a soft, rubbery shell covered in a leathery skin. It is also the most widely dispersed sea turtle, living in every ocean in the globe and migrating thousands of kilometres annually in search of food and beaches for nesting. The leatherback turtle has been a remarkable survivor for over 100 million years, but because to human activities including egg harvesting, bycatch in fishing gear, and plastic pollution, it is now thought to be critically endangered. Protection of this unique animal and its environment is being pursued through conservation efforts.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

(Caretta caretta)

The big species of marine turtle known as the loggerhead turtle, or Caretta, is distinguished by its large head and strong jaw. It may grow up to 3.5 feet long and weigh 350 pounds, and it can be found in both temperate and tropical waters all around the world. The loggerhead turtle is a species with a long lifespan that can last up to 50 years in the wild. It is renowned for its exceptional navigation skills, returning to the same nesting beaches year after year. Despite being a species that is thought to be in danger due to habitat degradation, unintentional entanglement in fishing gear, and other human activities, conservation initiatives have assisted in raising populations in some places.

Olive Ridley Sea Turtle

(Lepidochelys olivacea)

The Pacific, Indian, and Atlantic Seas all have warm waters where the olive ridley sea turtle can be found. It is the second-smallest marine turtle, reaching a maximum length of around 2 feet, and can weigh up to 100 pounds. It is the most common sea turtle, with an estimated population of over 800,000 nesting females. This turtle is well-known for its extraordinary mass nesting occasions, known as arribadas, where hundreds of females congregate on one beach to deposit their eggs within a few days of one another. Unfortunately, these occurrences put the turtles in jeopardy since they are more open to poaching and unintentional fishing net capture.

About Us

Sea turtle safety and conservation are our top priorities at our turtle hatchery. We remove turtle eggs from unsafe nesting locations and carefully incubate them until they are mature enough to be returned to the ocean. Our goal is to teach people about sea turtle protection while also providing guided tours of our hatchery. We are proud of our dedication to environmentally friendly techniques and responsible tourism, and we work together with regional communities to further these practices. Come to our hatchery to help us safeguard these incredible creatures.
