Turtles of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a country with a very rich biodiversity. Surrounded by the Indian Ocean, it is home to many indigenous species of animals known to mankind. Sadly, as of today many of those are under threat of extinction, with most species sea turtles among them. As humans, it is our fault as well as our responsibility to help preserve the beautiful creatures.

Most Common Sea Turtles in Existence.

  • Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas)
  • Hawksbill Sea Turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata)
  • Leatherback Sea Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea)
  • Loggerhead Sea Turtle (Caretta caretta)
  • Olive Ridley Sea Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea)

With hundreds of turtles coming ashore each year to deposit their eggs on its beaches, Sri Lanka is one of the most significant sea turtle nesting locations in the Indian Ocean. However, due to human activities like beachfront development, overfishing, and pollution growth, Sri Lanka's turtle population has come under serious threat. These endangered species' survival is still in danger despite various conservation efforts.

Turtle hatcheries that rescue and incubate eggs and release the hatchlings back into the ocean are among the organizations and programs striving to safeguard and conserve the turtle population in Sri Lanka. To safeguard these amazing animals and their ecosystems, we must keep spreading awareness and taking all necessary actions

3 Main Turtle Conservation Centers in SL.

  1. Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery
  2. Sea Turtle Project, Bentota
  3. Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

About Us

Sea turtle safety and conservation are our top priorities at our turtle hatchery. We remove turtle eggs from unsafe nesting locations and carefully incubate them until they are mature enough to be returned to the ocean. Our goal is to teach people about sea turtle protection while also providing guided tours of our hatchery. We are proud of our dedication to environmentally friendly techniques and responsible tourism, and we work together with regional communities to further these practices. Come to our hatchery to help us safeguard these incredible creatures.
