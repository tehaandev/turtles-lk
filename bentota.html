Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Sea Turtle Project, Bentota is a conservation project located on the west coast of Sri Lanka. The hatchery is dedicated to the conservation of sea turtles by rescuing and incubating turtle eggs and releasing the hatchlings back into the ocean. The hatchery offers visitors an opportunity to learn about the different turtle species and their habitats through guided tours. In addition to its conservation efforts, the hatchery conducts outreach programs with local communities to raise awareness about the importance of protecting sea turtles and their habitats. As a vital player in the conservation of sea turtles in the region, Bentota turtle hatchery is committed to preserving the delicate balance of the coastal ecosystem and protecting the endangered species for future generations.

About Us

Sea turtle safety and conservation are our top priorities at our turtle hatchery. We remove turtle eggs from unsafe nesting locations and carefully incubate them until they are mature enough to be returned to the ocean. Our goal is to teach people about sea turtle protection while also providing guided tours of our hatchery. We are proud of our dedication to environmentally friendly techniques and responsible tourism, and we work together with regional communities to further these practices. Come to our hatchery to help us safeguard these incredible creatures.
